how can a letter sit on a page?

six experiments in how words can be shaped, revealed, and arranged when the page is treated as a surface and not a container. all text fresh, written for this sandbox.

01   unfold
scroll-driven ink
left → right per line
My dear self,
I am writing this from a kitchen that does not exist yet.
The window faces east. You will love that.
There is a plant on the sill I have been speaking to
the way you used to speak to your grandmother's piano.
It listens the same way. Patiently. Without judgment.
— from a slightly later morning
02   marginalia
a clean column with
a busy mind beside it

You will move to Paris in the autumn of your twenty-second year, and for the first three weeks you will be unbearably lonely. This is not a sign that you have chosen wrongly. It is the price of the door you have opened. Pay it.

You will meet a boy with a green wool scarf at a party in the eleventh and you will be certain, for almost two months, that he is the reason you came. He is not. He is the language teacher. You'll see.

When the homesickness arrives, and it will, do not try to outrun it. Walk to the bridge by the Institut and let it sit beside you for an hour. Then walk home and make eggs.

this passes.
i promise it does.
you will laugh
about him in 2027
the small one,
with the green door
with butter.
always with butter.
