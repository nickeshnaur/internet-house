Concordance

Thirty days. One word a day. Your composition.

Day 30 of 30 · "Compositions" · complete

The composition

— assembled from thirty days' words

I. mist ember ripple letter soundless glimmer
II. moth vellum harp unfold alone soften
III. attic threshold linen cedar parchment taper
IV. level softly sparrow november shawl ledger
V. signature tendril ghost midnight unbinding closing
— thirty days, thirty words, one composition

Read as prose

— the same thirty words, knit into sentences

A mist. An ember on the windowsill.
A ripple in the letter,
soundless — a glimmer
of the moth on vellum.

The harp's unfold.
Alone, the dusk softens
the attic — the threshold
of linen, of cedar,
of parchment and a taper
level with what's left.

Softly. A sparrow.
November. A shawl
folded over the ledger.

A signature. A tendril.
A ghost at midnight —
the unbinding,
the closing.

— the player's words in dark ink; the editor's connective tissue is grey
